Coronavirus Simulator


Welcome! This is a fun side project I've been working on during quarantine (learn more here). At times, social distancing can be draining and it's easy to feel jaded about the effects of this whole process, but coding this helps remind me why all of this is so important. Though I am not an epidemiologist and bouncing balls don't exactly represent human interaction, I found these tools fun to play with (and I hope you do too)! - Jude B.


Basic Social Distancing

This is the most generic simulation. You can control the amount of people who social distance, those who don't, and the starting infected population. Play around with flattening the curve!

Timed Social Distancing

As eager as we all are to stop social distancing, leaving too soon might also have detrimental impacts on the longevity of the virus. This allows you to play around with how long people social distance for, and see how duration affects infection.

Go to Simulation!
Physical Barriers

How do physical barriers between populations affect infection? This splits up a population evenly, keeping the infected people on the left. With one controllable opening to the other side, play around with how one case spreads to entire communities.

Go to Simulation!
Infection Rates

This simulation lets you alter the virus itself! Control how likely one is to get the virus from bouncing into someone else and how long someone is contagious for. This could also simulate how well a community responds to a virus (hand washing, wearing masks, coughing into shoulders, etc.. ).

Quarantining Populations

What happens when people are physically quarantined after being diagnosed? What if not everyone immediately showed symptomps? See how fast acting a total quarantine solution is. (Based off of this video.)
